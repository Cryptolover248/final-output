THE IVATANS

The northernmost Philippine islands of Batanes and Babuyan are home to the Austronesian ethnolinguistic group known as the Ivatan people. They share close linguistic and cultural links with the Tao people of Taiwan's Orchid Island and are genetically connected to other ethnic groups in Northern Luzon. The characteristics of the Ivatans are described as being sturdy and hardworking, but also peaceful, God-loving, honest, resilient, helpful, happy, and gentle. These characteristics were undoubtedly formed by the environment, religion, education, and their particular evolution as individuals.

FAQs
  • Who are allowed to go to Batanes?

    Only travelers who are fully vaccinated may enter Batanes for leisure purposes. Those ineligible for vaccination need to present proof of ineligibility like a medical certification or a birth certificate for children below 5 years old.

  • What are the entry points in Batanes?

    The main entry point in Batanes is Basco Airport, located in the provinceâ€™s capital. Most Philippine flights booked online to Basco Airport are from Manila.

  • What travel requirements do you need to prepare?

    Here are the requirements that you need to prepare before going to Batanes:

    a. Get a vaccination card or vaccination certificate (for fully-vaccinated)

    b. You need to show proof of your fully vaccinated status, and the Batanes local government unit accepts a valid vaccination card or a vaccination certificate from vaxcert.doh.gov.ph

    c. Secure proof of ineligibility for a COVID-19 vaccination (for unvaccinated)

    d. Parents or guardians of children below 5 years old need to present the birth certificate of their child to prove ineligibility. Those with medical conditions need to present a medical certificate.

    e. Book accommodations and tours with DOT-accredited establishments

    f. Prior reservation with any of the DOT-accredited Batanes hotels and resorts is a must, as well as booking with DOT-accredited tour operators. Do-it-yourself tours are not allowed. It's recommended to book airport transfers as well for a hassle-free arrival and departure.

  • What are the guidelines and protocols to follow when you're in Batanes?

    All residents and tourists must wear face masks, wash hands regularly, and practice proper social distancing, especially in public establishments, to help reduce the risk of community transmission of COVID-19 disease.

    Arrival protocols

    All tourists will undergo health screening upon arrival. Those exhibiting symptoms will be tested for free, and those who test positive for COVID-19 will be isolated in a government facility.

    Tour protocols

    Tourists are strongly discouraged from mingling with locals who are not part of their Batanes itinerary.

Official Government

Batanes is headed by Provincial Governor Marilou Cayco and Vice Governor Ignacio Villa. The Batanes Provincial Board, also known as the Sangguniang Panlalawigan, serves as its provincial legislature. The presidents of the provincial chapters of the Association of Barangay Captains, the Councilors' League, and the provincial president of Sangguniang Kabataan are also ex officio members.
