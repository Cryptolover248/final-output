PLACES TO GO

Ruins of Song Song

Ruins of Song song is a ghost barangay in Uyugan which features a cluster of roofless shells of old houses abandoned after the tidal wave that hit the island of Batan in the 50s and is a one hour road trip from Basco. It has a good stretch of beach recommended for swimming

Diura Fishing Village

This is a sitio in the eastern part of Mahatao town where an association of fishermen called Mataw practice the traditional fishing for Arayu (dorado). They have a ritual called kapayvanuvanua signifying the start of the fishing season which usually falls in March. The village offers a good view of the Pacific Ocean and Mt. Iraya.

Racuh-a-Payaman

It is the iconic hills and ocean view in Batanes a.k.a. Marlboro Hills. It is a promontory and a communal pastureland in the Barangay Imnajbu in the municipality of Uyugan, Southern part of Batan Island.

Old Loran Station

Old Loran Station in Uyugan used to house an old US Coast Guard detachment for almost two decades and is now converted to as a National Museum.

House of Dakkay

House of Dakkay is one of the oldest houses in the small town of San Jose de Ivana. This traditional structure is considered as a heritage treasure of Batanes

Mt. Matarem

Mt. Matarem is an extinct volcano 495 meters at its summit found in the municipality of Ivana. The name Matarem means “steep” because the trails of the mountain are really rugged, rough, and steep.

Honesty of Coffee Shop

The Honesty Coffee Shop situated near the port of Ivana, Batanes is a quaint self-service coffee shop and one of the most highly respected attractions in the province and in the world for showcasing the Ivatan's honesty.

San Jose Church

San Jose Church also known as the Ivana Church was built in 1814. The church was built with the intention of serving the people of Sabtang, Ivana, and Uyugan.

Chawa Cave

Chawa Cave in Mahatao is for the adventurous tripper. An enchanted cave with natural salt beds whose mouth opens to the South China Sea and is accessible through the boulders of Chawa Point.

San Carlos Borromeo Church

San Carlos Borromeo Church in Mahatao was completed in 1789 and still retains its centuries old features. It has been declared by the National Historical Institute as a Heritage Site and is one of the region’s National Cultural Treasure.

Valugan Bay

Valugan Bay in Basco has a kilometer-long stretch of beach with multi-colored and different sizes of rocks that helps to create an incredible landscape framed by almost vertical cliffs and rolling hills. It has a good view of the calming sea while the stones and rocks offer visitors to spend the hours away as they play.

Naidi Hills

It is an old sitio located in Basco and it offers a unique view of Baluarte Bay, Mount Iraya, and the rolling hills of Batan Island. The visitors will also see the American period wireless telegraph facilities that were bombed by the Japanese Imperial Army. One may also get a breathtaking view of Basco atop the lighthouse situated on top of the hill.

Mt. Iraya

Mt. Iraya is a dormant volcano located in Batan Island in the province of Batanes. It is also the highest point in Batanes with an elevation of 1,009 meters. Mount Iraya is a landmark of great importance and legend for the Ivatans.

Sabtang Island A Sight To Behold

It is one of the most beautiful islands in Batanes. It has intermittent sand beaches and is dominated with steep mountains and deep canyons with small level areas sporadically found along the coastline.

Official Government

Batanes is headed by Provincial Governor Marilou Cayco and Vice Governor Ignacio Villa. The Batanes Provincial Board, also known as the Sangguniang Panlalawigan, serves as its provincial legislature. The presidents of the provincial chapters of the Association of Barangay Captains, the Councilors' League, and the provincial president of Sangguniang Kabataan are also ex officio members.
